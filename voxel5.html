About Us

After 15 years of research in medical imaging analysis, Voxel 53 is a spin-off from NIDUS (Northern Institute for Deep Learning in Ultrasound), based in Edmonton, Alberta, Canada. We are primarily located in the Department of Radiology and Diagnostic Imaging, Faculty of Medicine & Dentistry, University of Alberta. Our aim is to share our expertise to make the most of medical imaging as it applies to digital health, by improving clinical workflows, enhancing outcomes, and providing a better patient care experience.

Our name reflects the basic component of any 3D medical image (the voxel, like a 3D pixel), and our origins in Edmonton, which is above the 53rd parallel in latitude.

Medical Imaging Solutions

The flagship product being developed with Voxel 53 assistance is Ultrasound Arm Injury Detect (US-AID), which aims to provide greater access to diagnostic imaging for the assessment of upper limb injuries. These injuries are currently assessed in emergency departments using X-ray, or by outpatient MRI, which involve long wait times. Our solution uses low-cost, pocket-sized ultrasound with images interpreted by AI technology to empower lightly trained users, such as nurses and clinic staff, to perform these examinations, thereby reducing wait times and increasing access to care for those who need it most.

We are currently evaluating and consulting on several other projects in earlier stages, including simplified acquisition of ultrasound images in many body parts, automated analysis of fetal cardiac ultrasound, automated breast ultrasound (ABUS), and MRI of arthritis at knees and temporomandibular joints (TMJ).

Educational Services in Med Tech

Voxel 53 aims to provide educational material that leads to more equitable access to healthcare. Our clinical team develops micro-certification modules for specific aspects of medical diagnostic imaging. These modules are designed to empower healthcare professionals with the knowledge and skills needed to utilize advanced imaging technologies effectively. By offering these educational resources, Voxel 53 strives to bridge the gap in healthcare and ensure that high-quality diagnostic tools are more widely accessible.

Our Team

Dr. Jacob Jaremko

Chief Medical Officer (CMO)

Dr. Jacob Jaremko is a Professor of Radiology and Adjunct Professor of Computing Science at the University of Alberta, a practicing board-certified Pediatric and Musculoskeletal radiologist and partner at Medical Imaging Consultants (mic.ca), and co-founder of 3 startup companies including MEDO.ai. He has a PhD in Biomedical Engineering. He is a Canada CIFAR AI Chair and Fellow of AMII, the Alberta Machine Intelligence Institute. His research has focused on developing objective imaging biomarkers of disease in ultrasound and MRI, and on implementing AI-augmented medical imaging diagnostic tools at the clinical point of care -- building the 21st-century stethoscope.

Dr. Abhilash Hareendranathan

Chief Technical Officer (CTO)

Dr. Abhilash Hareendranathan is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Radiology at the University of Alberta. His research focuses on artificial intelligence (AI), medical image analysis, and ultrasound imaging. With a diverse background, Dr. Hareendranathan has collaborated with research teams at Panasonic R&D Center in Singapore and Curefab in Germany. Prior to his academic role, he served as the R&D lead at MEDO.ai Inc., an AI start-up dedicated to developing innovative solutions for medical ultrasound. Throughout his career, Dr. Hareendranathan has pioneered AI approaches for automatic biomarker discovery, enhancing the speed and reliability of ultrasound assessments, and improving clinical workflows by empowering lightly trained users.
